# OHS-001: Health and Safety Policy

**Document ID:** OHS-001  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** January 4, 2026  
**Owner:** OHS Manager  
**ISO Standard:** ISO 45001:2018

---

## 1. Policy Statement

MyAeroTravel ID is committed to providing a safe and healthy workplace for all employees, contractors, guides, and passengers. We recognize that health and safety is everyone's responsibility.

**Our commitment**:
- Eliminate hazards and reduce OH&S risks
- Provide safe and healthy working conditions
- Ensure consultation and participation of workers
- Comply with legal and other requirements
- Continually improve OH&S performance

---

## 2. Safety Objectives

1. **Zero Fatalities**: No work-related deaths
2. **Injury Rate**: < 2 incidents per 100 trips
3. **Near-Miss Reporting**: ≥ 80% of near-misses reported
4. **Training Compliance**: 100% completion of safety training
5. **Risk Assessment**: 100% of trips have pre-trip risk assessment
6. **Emergency Response Time**: < 15 minutes to initial response

---

## 3. Scope

Applies to:
- All employees and contractors
- Tour guides and crew
- Office operations
- Field operations (trips and tours)
- Partner facilities and vehicles
- All business activities

---

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 Top Management
- Establish and maintain OH&S policy
- Allocate resources
- Review OH&S performance quarterly
- Lead safety culture

### 4.2 OHS Manager
- Implement and maintain OH&S management system
- Conduct risk assessments
- Investigate incidents
- Provide safety training
- Monitor compliance

### 4.3 Operations Managers
- Implement safety procedures
- Conduct daily safety checks
- Report hazards immediately
- Ensure equipment maintenance

### 4.4 Tour Guides
- Complete pre-trip risk assessments
- Conduct passenger safety briefings
- Use safety equipment properly
- Report incidents and near-misses
- Follow emergency procedures

### 4.5 All Workers
- Take reasonable care for own safety
- Comply with safety procedures
- Report hazards and incidents
- Participate in safety training
- Use PPE when required

---

## 5. Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment

### 5.1 Hazard Categories

**Marine Hazards**:
- Weather conditions (storms, high waves)
- Water conditions (currents, tides)
- Marine life (jellyfish, sea urchins)
- Equipment failure

**Operational Hazards**:
- Slips, trips, falls
- Manual handling injuries
- Heat stress and dehydration
- Fatigue

**Security Hazards**:
- Theft or robbery
- Civil unrest
- Medical emergencies
- Lost/missing persons

### 5.2 Risk Assessment Process

Conducted:
- Before new trips/destinations
- After incidents or near-misses
- When processes change
- Annual review (minimum)

Risk Matrix: Likelihood × Severity = Risk Level

---

## 6. Emergency Response

### 6.1 Emergency Types
- Medical emergency
- Man overboard
- Equipment failure
- Severe weather
- Security threat

### 6.2 SOS Alert System
- Panic button on Guide App
- GPS location tracking
- Auto-notify emergency contacts
- Real-time updates to operations center

### 6.3 Emergency Contacts
- Operations Center: 24/7 hotline
- Local Coast Guard
- Hospital/Medical facilities
- Insurance emergency line

---

## 7. Incident Reporting

All incidents, accidents, and near-misses must be reported within:
- **Fatal/Serious**: Immediately
- **Minor injury**: Within 24 hours
- **Near-miss**: Within 48 hours

See OHS-004 for detailed procedures.

---

## 8. Safety Training

Required for all guides:
- Initial safety induction
- First Aid & CPR certification
- Emergency response procedures
- Risk assessment training
- Annual refresher training

---

## 9. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Required PPE based on activity:
- Life jackets (all water activities)
- Sun protection (hats, sunscreen)
- Appropriate footwear
- First aid kits (on all trips)
- Communication devices

---

## 10. Worker Consultation

Mechanisms:
- Monthly safety meetings
- Incident investigation participation
- Safety suggestion program
- Anonymous reporting system
- OH&S committee representation
