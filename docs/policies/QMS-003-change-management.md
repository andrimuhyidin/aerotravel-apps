## 1. Purpose

To establish a structured process for managing changes to products, services, processes, and systems to ensure quality, safety, and compliance are maintained while enabling continuous improvement.

---

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to changes affecting:
- Business processes and procedures
- IT systems and software
- Travel products and services
- Organizational structure
- Quality Management System
- Safety and security protocols
- Environmental management practices

---

## 3. Change Categories

### 3.1 By Impact

| Category | Definition | Approval Level | Examples |
|----------|------------|----------------|----------|
| **Minor** | Limited impact, low risk | Process Owner | Form updates, editorial changes |
| **Major** | Significant impact, medium risk | Department Head | Process redesign, new tools |
| **Critical** | Organization-wide, high risk | Executive Management | System replacement, policy changes |

### 3.2 By Type

- **Corrective**: Fix identified problems
- **Preventive**: Prevent potential problems
- **Improvement**: Enhance performance
- **Regulatory**: Compliance requirement
- **Emergency**: Urgent safety/security issue

---

## 4. Change Management Process

### 4.1 Process Flow

```
1. Identify → 2. Request → 3. Assess → 4. Approve → 5. Plan → 
6. Implement → 7. Verify → 8. Close → 9. Review
```

### 4.2 Detailed Steps

#### Step 1: Identify Need
- Problem identification
- Improvement opportunity
- Regulatory requirement
- Customer feedback
- Audit finding

#### Step 2: Submit Request
- Complete Change Request Form
- Describe current state and desired state
- Justify business need
- Attach supporting documents

#### Step 3: Impact Assessment
Quality team evaluates:
- Scope and scale of change
- Resources required
- Risk assessment
- Dependencies
- Affected stakeholders
- Training needs
- Documentation updates

#### Step 4: Approval
- Review by approval authority
- Risk vs. benefit analysis
- Resource allocation approval
- Timeline agreement
- Conditions/constraints documented

#### Step 5: Planning
- Detailed implementation plan
- Task assignments
- Timeline/milestones
- Communication plan
- Training plan
- Rollback plan (if applicable)

#### Step 6: Implementation
- Execute according to plan
- Monitor progress
- Document activities
- Manage issues
- Communicate status

#### Step 7: Verification
- Test changes
- Verify objectives met
- Validate documentation updated
- Confirm training completed
- Check for unintended consequences

#### Step 8: Closure
- Final approval
- Lessons learned captured
- Change log updated
- Stakeholders notified
- Metrics established

#### Step 9: Post-Implementation Review
- Effectiveness evaluation (30-90 days)
- Performance against objectives
- Benefits realization
- Corrective actions (if needed)

---

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

### 5.1 Change Requestor
- Submit complete change request
- Provide justification and evidence
- Support impact assessment
- Participate in implementation

### 5.2 Change Coordinator (Quality Team)
- Receive and log requests
- Coordinate impact assessment
- Schedule change review meetings
- Track change status
- Maintain change register

### 5.3 Change Advisory Board (CAB)
Members: Department Heads, Quality Manager, IT Manager

Responsibilities:
- Review major/critical changes
- Assess risks and dependencies
- Approve/reject/defer changes
- Prioritize competing changes
- Resolve conflicts

Meeting frequency: Bi-weekly

### 5.4 Approval Authority
- Review assessment and recommendation
- Make approval decision
- Allocate resources
- Set conditions/constraints

### 5.5 Implementation Team
- Execute approved changes
- Follow implementation plan
- Report progress/issues
- Complete verification
- Document results

## 6. Risk Assessment

### 6.1 Risk Evaluation Criteria

**Likelihood**:
- Low (1): Unlikely to occur
- Medium (2): May occur
- High (3): Likely to occur

**Impact**:
- Low (1): Minor disruption
- Medium (2): Moderate disruption
- High (3): Significant disruption

**Risk Level** = Likelihood × Impact

| Risk Score | Risk Level | Action |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| 1-2 | Low | Standard process |
| 3-4 | Medium | Enhanced monitoring |
| 6-9 | High | Executive approval required |

### 6.2 Mitigation Strategies

For medium/high risk changes:
- Pilot testing
- Phased rollout
- Enhanced communication
- Training programs
- Rollback procedures
- Contingency plans

---

## 7. Emergency Changes

### 7.1 Criteria

Emergency changes may be expedited when:
- Critical safety issue identified
- Security breach detected
- System failure affecting operations
- Regulatory compliance at risk

### 7.2 Process

1. **Verbal approval** from appropriate authority
2. **Immediate implementation** with documentation
3. **Formal change request** submitted within 24 hours
4. **Retrospective review** at next CAB meeting

### 8.2 Communication Templates

- Completion notification

## 9. Documentation Requirements

### 9.1 Change Request Form

Must include:
- Change ID and date
- Requestor details
- Change description

## 10. Training and Awareness

### 10.1 Training Requirements

When changes affect:
- **Processes**: Training before go-live
- **Systems**: Hands-on training sessions
- **Procedures**: Review sessions
- **Policies**: Awareness communications

### 10.2 Training Documentation

- Training materials prepared
- Training sessions conducted
- Attendance recorded
- Competence assessed

## 11. Monitoring and Metrics

### 11.1 Key Performance Indicators

| KPI | Target | Frequency |
|-----|--------|-----------|
| Change success rate | ≥ 95% | Monthly |
| Average approval time | ≤ 5 business days | Weekly |
| Changes requiring rollback | < 5% | Monthly |
| Overdue changes | < 10% | Weekly |
| Stakeholder satisfaction | ≥ 4.0/5.0 | Per change |

### 11.2 Reporting

- Monthly change management report
- Quarterly trend analysis
- Annual effectiveness review
